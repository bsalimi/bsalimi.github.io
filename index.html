Biography

I am an incoming Assistant Professor in the Halıcıoğlu Data Science Institute (HDSI)(https://datascience.ucsd.edu/) at University of of Califonia, San Diego(UCSD)(https://ucsd.edu/)

I am a research associate at University of Washington and part of UW Database Group, where I work with Prof. Dan Suciu. I completed my Ph.D. in 2015 at Carleton University, advised by Prof. Leopoldo Bertossi.

My research seeks to unify techniques from theoretical data management, causal inference and machine learning (ML) to develop a new generation of decision-support systems that help people with heterogeneous backgrounds make sound decisions using {\em prescriptive analytics}. My ongoing work in {\em causal inference from relational data} aims to develop the necessary conceptual foundations to make causal inference and counterfactual analysis accessible to non-statisticians. Further, my research in the area of {\em algorithmic fairness} develops needed foundations for ensuring {\em fairness} in data-driven decision making. Further, my research contributions have been recognized with a Postdoc Reserach Award at UW, {\bf Best Demonstration Paper Award} at VLDB 2018 and a {\bf Best Paper Award} in SIGMOD 2019, UW Postdoc Reserach Award.

I am actively looking for postdoc fellow, postgraduate students, and PhD students for Fall 2020. I have multiple openings in the research areas listed above. Prospective postdocs and students should email me and explain their research interests and how they relate to my work.
